In composition, silence is well known as a musical element, rendering a particular frequency of sound (its absence), an accompanying duration of sound, or of silence. Silence does not simply represent a blank or negative space in music.

Articulate Silence Industries seeks to find beauty in the seemingly mundane. In Issue 01 (or Magnetic Tape 01, referring to loops of magnetic tape used to create repetitive, rhythmic musical patterns), we explore the possibilities of chiastic ABCDCBA in musical composition that is used in Steve Reich's Music for 18 Musicians by playing a couple of the samples (or as we call here, Magnetic Storage, referring to the storage of data on a magnetized medium) in the drum machine. For this issue, each of the performance that is being performed by at least two people, will be recorded for exactly 18 seconds.
