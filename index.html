In Issue 01 (or Magnetic Tape, referring to loops of magnetic tape used to create repetitive, rhythmic musical patterns), we explore the possibilities of chiastic ABCDCBA in musical composition that is used in Steve Reich's Music for 18 Musicians by playing a couple of the samples (or as we call here, Magnetic Storage, referring to the storage of data on a magnetized medium) in the drum machine.

The samples were taken from various experimental musicians and we define experimental music as music in which the composer introduces the elements of chance or unpredictability with regard to either the composition or its performance.

For this issue, each of the performance that is being performed by at least two people, will be recorded for exactly 18 seconds.
In composition, silence is well known as a musical element, rendering a particular frequency of sound (its absence), an accompanying duration of sound, or of silence. Articulate Silence Industries seeks to find beauty in the seemingly mundane.
